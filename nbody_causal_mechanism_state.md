## 1. Current claim (one paragraph)

In concentrated stylised N-body systems (cusp and core), the future **central concentration** is
causally controlled by **how many particles are on low-pericenter orbits** — the fraction
`P(r_peri < r_c)` — *not* by the coarse-graining scale, the global mean angular momentum ⟨|L|⟩,
the velocity anisotropy β, the total energy, or the virial ratio. Lowering the angular momentum of
the **inner** orbits (the ones already near `r_c`) makes them reach small pericenters, growing
`M(<r_c)`, which in turn drives clustering at the **same scale**. This is established by matched-pair
interventions with antisymmetric, time-ordered, form-robust, cross-profile, particle-number-robust,
shell-localised, and **dose-validated** evidence.

## 4. Current causal chain

```
   Δ P(r_peri < r_c)   →   Δ M(<r_c)   →   Δ C8
   (low-pericenter         (central        (clustering,
    inner-orbit            concentration)   scale-matched)
    population)
```

**Caveat (important and real):** the `ΔM(<r_c) → ΔC₈` link is **scale-matched, not automatic.**
Very-inner concentration can rise while the 0.25-grid C₈ *falls* — e.g. inner-shell radialization
gives ΔC₈ ≈ −0.4…−1.3 while mid/full give +4…+4.7 — because radial inner orbits plunge *through* the
centre and oscillate out, concentrating the core but smoothing mass at the C₈ grid scale. C₈ must be
read at its own scale (cell ≈ L/8 = 0.25).

Anisotropy β sits *beside* this chain as a transient downstream signature of the orbital
reconfiguration, useful as a diagnostic but not the cause.

## 5. Definitions

- **Pericenter `r_peri`** (effective-potential inner turning point). Per particle: specific energy
  `E = ½|v|² + Φ(r)`, specific angular momentum `|L| = r·v_t` (v_t = tangential speed). Potential
  proxy `Φ(r) = −GM/(r+a)` (Hernquist, G=M=1, a=0.20). `r_peri` = smallest `r` where the radial
  kinetic term `v_r²(r) = 2(E − Φ(r)) − L²/r² ≥ 0`, found by a 300-point log grid scan over
  r∈[0.005, 5]. (Analytic-potential proxy; see open question on a self-consistent / depth-weighted
  version.) Implemented in `nbody_orbital_summary.py::_pericenters`.
- **Concentration-relevant orbit population:** `f_peri(r_c) = fraction of particles with r_peri < r_c`.
- **`r_c` values tested:** **0.05, 0.10, 0.20** (with a = 0.20, so r_c/a ∈ {0.25, 0.5, 1.0}).
- **Concentration target:** `M(<r_c)` = fraction of particles within `r_c` of the COM.
- **Clustering target:** `C₈` = variance of particle counts on an 8³ grid (cell = L/8 = 0.25);
  extensive (~N). **`ΔC₈` per intervention is read for sign/scale, not absolute magnitude across N.**
- **Interventions** (all speed-preserving → KE/E/Q conserved): *radialize* (rotate v toward nearest
  radial axis), *tangentialize* (toward the tangential plane), *v_t-rescale* (multiply v_t by f<1,
  raise |v_r|), *shell radialize* (radialize only an r-percentile band), *sham* (random-axis
  rotation, magnitude-matched).

## 7. Scope (what the claim covers)

- **Systems:** concentrated, spherically-ish stylised ICs — **Hernquist (cusp)** and **Plummer
  (core)**. Both pass.
- **Contrast:** **uniform3d** (non-concentrated box) shows a *different, passive* mechanism with a
  reversed clustering sign → concentration is required.
- **Model:** `direct_isolated` (softened direct summation) — the only model where ε affects the
  force (PM is ε-invariant). N = 1024 default; **N = 512–2048** for the resolution check.
- **Not covered / not claimed:** cosmological haloes or galaxy populations; a continuum / Vlasov
  (N→∞) statement; non-concentrated, rotating, or strongly aspherical systems; potentials beyond
  the tested two; a thermodynamic limit.

## 8. Open questions (ranked)

1. **Depth-weighted pericenter variable** — replace the binary `f_peri = #(r_peri<r_c)/N` with a
   weighted `Σ_i w(r_peri,i)` (e.g. weighting by pericenter depth or time-inside-r_c). The ~15–20%
   residual scatter in the dose-response (mid/full deviate) points here. **Highest-value next run.**
2. **Plummer dose-response** — Plummer has the handle + timing but not the *graded* dose; repeat the
   dose-response (`nbody_pericenter_dose_response.py` with `plummer3d`).
3. **Longer-time asymptotic memory** — behaviour beyond t≈1000 (does concentration saturate, decay?).
4. **Larger N / Vlasov-like limit** — push N upward to test the continuum approach (the intensive
   effect is flat to N=2048; how far does that hold?).
5. **Action-space formulation** — express the causal variable in actions (J_r, L) rather than the
   pericenter proxy, for a potential-independent statement.

## 9. Kill-test constitution (binding on all future claims)

1. **Target-self baseline.** Any correlational claim about a future delta target (e.g. ΔC₈, Δβ,
   Δσ_r) **must** control for the initial value of that target's own quantity. Two branches died to
   this trap (C₈, phase-space). Prefer **matched-pair interventions** over correlation.
2. **Global summaries are insufficient.** A global scalar (⟨|L|⟩, β, virial, energy) may not be
   claimed causal unless it survives a **shell-/pericenter-localised** test. The global ⟨|L|⟩
   failure is the precedent: it can move a lot while the outcome does not.
3. **C₈ at its own scale.** C₈ probes the L/8 ≈ 0.25 grid; it can dissociate from very-inner M(<r_c).
   Never treat "concentration" and "C₈" as interchangeable without matching scales.
4. **Mandatory controls.** Sham (magnitude-matched), KE/Q drift (rule out bulk/energy contamination),
   and a free-streaming / no-dynamics baseline (rule out ballistic carryover) on every causal run.
5. **Conservation by construction beats post-hoc.** Speed-preserving interventions keep E/Q fixed *a
   priori*; report the measured drift, but design for ≈0.
6. **Local first.** Every overclaim in this branch was caught by a small local experiment, never by
   sample size. Spend local runs before any AWS-scale campaign; AWS is not the current bottleneck.
