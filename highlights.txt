Highlights

• Coarse density variance robustly dominates prediction for merging binary clusters across all tested N, ε, and force models.
• No positional fine-scale observable outperforms the coarse predictor in any tested cell.
• Local velocity dispersion shows a restricted fine-kinematic advantage for concentrated cusp profiles at low softening.
• The fine-kinematic advantage erodes with increasing ε and disappears by ε = 0.10.
• Predictive advantage depends on initial-condition family, observable class, and softening-to-scale-radius ratio ε/a.
